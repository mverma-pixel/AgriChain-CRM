Getting Started

Logging In

Navigate to the CRM URL and log in with your username and password. Contact your admin if you don't have an account.

Navigation

Use the left sidebar to navigate between modules: Dashboard, Leads, Contacts, Companies, Calendar, Tasks, Reports, and Settings.

Click the search button in the sidebar to search across all leads, contacts, and companies.

Leads

What is a Lead?

A lead is a potential customer who has shown interest in AgriChain. Leads move through a defined pipeline of stages.

Lead Stages

  • New Lead — Just entered the system, not yet qualified.
  • SAL — Sales Accepted Lead: qualified and ready for outreach.
  • Out — Lead is not progressing (requires reason).
  • Demo — A product demo has been scheduled or completed.
  • Close Won — Deal closed successfully.
  • Close Lost — Deal lost (requires reason).

Why is a reason required for Out and Close Lost?

To build a data-driven understanding of why leads don't convert, we require a written reason whenever a lead is marked as Out or Close Lost — or when moving away from those stages. This feedback feeds directly into the Reports section.

Auto-Assignment

Leads from Australia and New Zealand are automatically assigned to Skye. Leads from USA and Canada go to Tony. All others are Unassigned. You can always override assignment manually.

Contacts & Companies

Contacts

Contacts represent real people you interact with. A contact can be created manually, converted from a lead, or created via the API.

Converting a Lead to a Contact

On any lead detail page, click "Convert to Contact". This creates a Contact record from the lead's data and links them together.

Companies

Companies represent organisations. Contacts and Leads can be linked to a company. Use the Companies module to track all your key accounts.

Tasks & Calendar

Tasks

Tasks represent action items. Each task has a title, priority (Low/Medium/High), due date, assignee, and optional link to a lead, contact, or company.

Use the "My Tasks" tab to see only your own tasks. The "Overdue" tab shows tasks that have passed their due date.

Calendar

The Calendar shows all scheduled events by month. Click any day to see the events for that day in the right panel. Add events using the "+ Add Event" button.

Event types: Call, Meeting, Demo, Follow-up, Other — each shown in a different colour.

Reports

  • Lead Funnel — Visual breakdown of leads at each stage with average time spent.
  • Source Performance — Which marketing channels drive the most conversions.
  • Team Performance — How Skye, Tony, and Unassigned leads compare.
  • Activity Report — Full audit log of all CRM activity, filterable by user and date.
  • Funnel Leak Analysis — Where leads are dropping out of the funnel.

API

Webhook — Create a Lead

POST to /api/webhook/lead with a JSON body. No authentication required for the webhook endpoint.

curl -X POST https://your-crm.agrichain.com/api/webhook/lead \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "name": "Jane Smith",
    "company": "FarmCo",
    "country": "Australia",
    "email": "jane@farmco.com",
    "phone": "+61400000000",
    "message": "Interested in grain tracking",
    "source_channel": "Homepage",
    "source_url": "https://agrichain.com/features/grain-tracking"
  }'

Slack Integration

Setup

  1. In Slack, go to Apps → Incoming Webhooks → Add to Slack.
  2. Choose the channel you want notifications posted to.
  3. Copy the Webhook URL.
  4. In AgriChain CRM, go to Settings → Integrations.
  5. Paste the URL and click Save Webhook.
  6. Click Test Slack to verify it works.

What triggers Slack notifications?

  • New lead created
  • Lead marked Close Won
  • Lead marked Close Lost (includes reason)
  • Daily 9:00 AM breach alert for leads not updated in 7+ days
